BIOLOGICAL INTERPRETATION
-------------------------
Allochromatium vinosum is a purple sulfur bacterium that uses light energy
to oxidize sulfide to sulfur/sulfate via the reverse-dsr pathway. It fixes
CO2 via the Calvin-Benson cycle and can also fix N2. The qmoA discriminator
correctly classifies this organism as a phototrophic sulfide oxidizer rather
than a sulfate reducer, despite having strong dsrAB sequence homology.

Recommended medium: Modified Pfennig's medium for purple sulfur bacteria
  - NaHCO3 buffer (CO2 source)
  - Na2S·9H2O (1-2 mM, electron donor)
  - NH4Cl (nitrogen source)
  - Trace element solution SL-10
  - Light at 600-1000 nm (infrared/near-infrared)
  - Anaerobic atmosphere (N2/CO2 80:20)
  - pH 7.0-7.5, 25-30°C
